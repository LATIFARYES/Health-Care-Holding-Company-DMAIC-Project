
04 — Change Management
Health Holding Company | Improve Phase

Purpose: To manage the human side of Emergency Department process improvement and ensure operational changes are adopted successfully and sustained over time.

Most operational improvement failures occur because of resistance to change — not because the solution itself is incorrect.

ADKAR Model Application 🔄

The ADKAR model is used to guide Emergency Department staff through the transition toward the new operational workflows.

| Stage | Objective | Our Approach |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| Awareness | Why are we changing? | Share Analyze phase findings showing overcrowding, long waiting times, and patient delays |
| Desire | What's in it for me? | Explain how Fast Track and smart staffing reduce workload pressure and congestion |
| Knowledge | How do I do it? | Conduct hands-on training for triage, routing, and dashboard workflows |
| Ability | Can I do it? | Run pilot testing and supervised implementation support |
| Reinforcement | How do we sustain it? | Use KPI dashboards, daily huddles, and recognition of improvement results |

Change Management Strategy 🏥
Awareness

Staff will be informed about:

Current waiting-time performance
Patient overcrowding impact
Risks to patient satisfaction and safety
Why operational change is necessary
Desire

Operational teams will be engaged early by:

Involving frontline staff in workflow design
Gathering physician and nurse feedback
Demonstrating workload improvements
Explaining benefits to patient care and staff efficiency
Knowledge

Training will include:

Fast Track workflow
Standardized triage process
Queue dashboard usage
Patient-routing guidelines
Escalation procedures
Ability

Staff will practice improvements during:

Pilot implementation
Simulation sessions
Shift-based operational coaching
Real-time supervision during rollout
Reinforcement

Sustainability actions include:

Daily operational huddles
KPI visibility boards
Monthly performance reviews
Recognition of successful teams
Continuous feedback collection
Resistance Diagnosis (COM-B Model) 🧠

If staff revert to old workflows, the issue is treated as a system or behavioral barrier — not personal failure.

| COM-B Area | Example in Emergency Department | Action |
|-------------|--------------------------------|--------|
| Capability Issue | Staff unsure how to use Fast Track workflow | Additional training and coaching |
| Opportunity Issue | Dashboard not easily visible during shifts | Improve workstation setup and visibility |
| Motivation Issue | Staff believe old workflow is faster | Reinforce pilot results and operational benefits |

Common Sources of Resistance ⚠️

| Resistance Source | Concern | Mitigation Strategy |
|-------------------|---------|---------------------|
| Physicians | Workflow disruption | Involve physicians in process design |
| Nurses | Increased workload concerns | Clarify staffing support and workflow simplification |
| Triage Staff | Fear of protocol complexity | Provide simplified guidelines and coaching |
| Bed Management | Increased coordination demands | Define clear escalation workflows |
| Operational Teams | Resistance to new systems | Use phased rollout and pilot testing |
