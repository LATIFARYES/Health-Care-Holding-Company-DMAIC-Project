
01 — Solutions Selection
Health Holding Company | Improve Phase

Purpose: To systematically evaluate proposed operational improvements and select the most effective solutions for reducing Emergency Department overcrowding, waiting times, and patient-flow delays using data-driven decision-making.

Methodology 📊
1. Impact / Effort Matrix (Quick Prioritization)

All proposed solutions are evaluated using a 2x2 prioritization matrix:

| Quadrant | Description | Action |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Quick Wins | High Impact, Low Effort | Implement immediately |
| Major Projects | High Impact, High Effort | Plan and phase carefully |
| Fill-ins | Low Impact, Low Effort | Implement if resources allow |
| Avoid | Low Impact, High Effort | Do not prioritize |

2. Solution Evaluation Matrix (Detailed Review)

Solutions are evaluated based on weighted operational criteria:

| Evaluation Criteria | Weight |
|---------------------|--------|
| Operational Impact | 40% |
| Ease of Implementation | 25% |
| Staff Acceptance | 20% |
| Sustainability | 15% |

Selected Solutions for Emergency Department 🚨

| Root Cause | Selected Solution | Matrix Quadrant | Justification |
|-------------|------------------|-----------------|--------------|
| RC-01: No Fast Track process | Fast Track pathway for low-acuity patients | Quick Win | High impact on overcrowding with moderate implementation effort |
| RC-02: Staffing shortages during peak hours | Smart staffing based on patient-volume analysis | Major Project | Requires scheduling redesign but significantly reduces delays |
| RC-03: Admission and bed delays | Bed coordination escalation process | Major Project | High impact on LOS and congestion |
| RC-04: No real-time queue visibility | Live operational dashboard | Quick Win | Immediate visibility improves operational response |
| RC-05: Workflow variation between shifts | Standardized triage protocol | Quick Win | Reduces variation with low operational cost |
| RC-06: No routing protocol | Standardized patient-routing workflow | Quick Win | Improves patient movement and reduces bottlenecks |

Behavioral Design Considerations 🧠

Operational improvements will fail without staff adoption and workflow integration.

| Solution | Behavioral Focus |
|-----------|------------------|
| Fast Track pathway | Reduce resistance by involving frontline teams |
| Smart staffing | Align staffing with actual workload patterns |
| Queue dashboard | Make operational status visible and actionable |
| Standardized triage | Reduce decision variation between shifts |
| Bed escalation process | Improve interdepartmental coordination |

Key Selection Principles ✅


Prioritize operational impact over complexity


Focus on solutions that directly address confirmed root causes


Select improvements that can be piloted quickly


Include frontline staff in workflow redesign


Balance immediate wins with long-term sustainability
