**Project Charter** 💼

**Project Title 📋:**

Emergency Department Waiting Time and Patient Flow Improvement


**Business Case 📌**

(Why this matters, What is the problem? What is the impact?)

The Emergency Department is experiencing severe overcrowding, extended patient waiting times, and inefficient patient flow. Door-to-Doctor Time currently averages 95 minutes, significantly above the ≤45-minute target. Length of Stay has reached 4.6 hours, while the percentage of patients leaving without being seen has increased to 8%.

Operationally, minor cases are consuming physician capacity intended for urgent and critical patients, creating congestion throughout the Emergency Department. Peak-hour staffing imbalance and the absence of an effective Fast Track pathway further increase delays and staff workload pressure.

Financially and operationally, these issues lead to inefficient resource utilization, increased overtime pressure, reduced patient satisfaction, and potential quality-performance penalties. Addressing these problems is critical to improving patient safety, restoring operational efficiency, and enhancing overall quality of care.


**Problem Statement (Data-Based) 📊**

Current Door-to-Doctor Time is 95 minutes compared to a target of ≤45 minutes, while Length of Stay is 4.6 hours compared to a target of ≤3 hours. Additionally, Left Without Being Seen (LWBS) has reached 8% compared to a target of ≤2%.

This has resulted in Emergency Department overcrowding, delays in critical patient care, increased staff workload, and reduced patient satisfaction scores of 78%.

**Goal Statement (SMART) 🎯**

Reduce Door-to-Doctor Time from 95 minutes to ≤45 minutes within 3 months
Reduce Length of Stay from 4.6 hours to ≤3 hours within 3 months
Reduce LWBS from 8% to ≤2% within 6 months
Increase Patient Satisfaction from 78% to ≥90% within 3 months
Reduce Triage Time from 15 minutes to ≤5 minutes within 3 months
