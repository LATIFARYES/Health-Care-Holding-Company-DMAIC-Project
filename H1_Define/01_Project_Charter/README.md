**What Is a Project Charter? 📑**

A Project Charter is a formal agreement between the project team and hospital leadership. It explains:

Why is the project needed? (Business Case)
What is the current problem? (Problem Statement)
What is the target outcome? (Goal Statement)
What processes are included? (Scope)
Who is responsible? (Project Team)
When will the project be completed? (Timeline)

**Key Sections Explained 🔍** 

Business Case

The business case explains why the project is important and why resources should be allocated.

The Emergency Department is currently experiencing long patient waiting times, overcrowding, and delays in treatment. These issues negatively impact patient satisfaction, operational efficiency, and quality of care.

**The project is expected to:**

Reduce patient waiting time
Improve patient flow efficiency
Increase patient satisfaction
Reduce overcrowding in critical care pathways
Improve staff workload balance


**Problem Statement** 

The Emergency Department has experienced excessive waiting times and overcrowding since January 2026.

**Current performance:**

Door-to-Doctor Time = 95 minutes
Length of Stay (LOS) = 4.6 hours
Left Without Being Seen = 8%
Patient Satisfaction = 78%

Target performance:

Door-to-Doctor Time ≤ 45 minutes
LOS ≤ 3 hours
LWBS ≤ 2%
Patient Satisfaction ≥ 90%

This delay is causing operational congestion, reduced patient satisfaction, increased staff pressure, and risk to critical patient care. The root causes are currently under investigation.


**Goal Statement 🎯**

Reduce Emergency Department waiting time from 95 minutes to ≤ 45 minutes by implementing patient segmentation, Fast Track pathways, smart staffing, and real-time monitoring.

**The project aims to:**

Improve patient flow
Reduce overcrowding
Increase operational efficiency
Improve patient satisfaction
Enhance quality of emergency care

**Best Practices ✅**

**Do's ⤵️**

Define measurable KPIs before implementation

Establish clear triage pathways

Monitor waiting times continuously

Pilot test solutions before full rollout

Maintain management support throughout the project


**Don'ts⤵️**

Do not implement solutions without baseline data

Do not mix critical and minor patient pathways

Do not ignore peak-hour staffing challenges

Do not expand scope beyond Emergency Department operations

Do not stop monitoring after implementation


**Common Mistakes ⚠️**
| Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Weak triage separation | Critical patient delays | Standardize triage criteria |
| No Fast Track staffing | Minor cases block patient flow | Assign dedicated staff |
| Poor staffing allocation | Overcrowding during peak hours | Use demand-based scheduling |
| No KPI monitoring | Problems go unnoticed | Use real-time dashboards |
| No control plan | Improvements fade over time | Establish continuous monitoring |
