Dear Hospital Leadership Team,

I am writing to formally announce the launch of the Emergency Department Waiting Time and Patient Flow Improvement Project, initiated in response to the operational performance gaps identified during the past several months.

Why this project matters:

The Emergency Department is currently experiencing significant overcrowding and extended patient waiting times. Current Door-to-Doctor Time averages 95 minutes against a target of ≤45 minutes, while Length of Stay has reached 4.6 hours compared to the ≤3-hour target. In addition, the Left Without Being Seen (LWBS) rate has increased to 8%, and patient satisfaction has decreased to 78%.

These issues are directly impacting patient experience, operational efficiency, staff workload, and the timely treatment of urgent and critical cases.

What we are doing:

We are launching a structured DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) improvement project focused on:

* Reducing Emergency Department waiting times
* Improving patient flow efficiency
* Implementing Fast Track pathways for minor cases
* Optimizing staffing during peak hours
* Enhancing real-time operational visibility

We are currently in the Define phase, where the project charter, process maps, stakeholder analysis, and Voice of Customer requirements are being completed.

What we need from you:

* Formal sponsorship and executive support for the project
* Approval to access Emergency Department operational and KPI data
* Support in allocating time for key stakeholders and frontline teams
* Assistance in resolving organizational barriers during implementation
* Continued engagement during project review and tollgate meetings
