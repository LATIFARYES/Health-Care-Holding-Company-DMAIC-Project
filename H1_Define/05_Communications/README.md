Best Practices for DMAIC Communications ✅

**Practice 1:**

Lead with Operational Impact

Every communication should answer:

“Why does this matter?”

Examples:

Management → Improved hospital KPIs and patient satisfaction

Physicians → Reduced overcrowding and smoother workflow

Nurses → Better workload balance and patient prioritization

Patients → Faster treatment and shorter waiting times

**Practice 2:**

Be Transparent About the Process

Explain DMAIC clearly and simply

Clarify that the project focuses on process improvement, not blame

Share timelines, milestones, and expected outcomes

**Practice 3:**

Create Two-Way Communication

Encourage feedback during meetings and pilot phases

Respond quickly to concerns and operational issues

Document feedback and actions taken

**Practice 4:** 

Tailor the Message to the Audience

| Audience | Language Style | Focus | Detail Level |
|-----------|----------------|-------|---------------|
| Hospital Management | Strategic / KPI-focused | ROI and operational performance | High-level |
| Emergency Department Leaders | Operational | Patient flow and staffing | Medium |
| Physicians & Nurses | Practical | Workflow impact | Detailed |
| Patients & Families | Service-focused | Waiting time and communication | Simple |

**Practice 5:**

Document All Communications

Save all meeting notes and emails

Maintain a communication log

Track concerns, decisions, and actions

Use documentation during DMAIC tollgate reviews

**Communication Templates 📧**

**Project Kickoff Email Template**

Dear Team,

We are launching a DMAIC improvement project focused on reducing Emergency Department waiting times, improving patient flow, and enhancing patient satisfaction.

Project Goals:

* Reduce Door-to-Doctor Time from 95 minutes to ≤45 minutes
* Reduce Length of Stay from 4.6 hours to ≤3 hours
* Improve Patient Satisfaction from 78% to ≥90%

Timeline:
July 1, 2026 – December 31, 2026

Your support will be essential during data collection, workflow analysis, and pilot implementation phases.

A kickoff meeting will be scheduled to review the project charter, objectives, and next steps.

Thank you for your cooperation and support.

Best regards,
Project Lead

Common Mistakes in Project Communication ⚠️

| Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Over-communicating with low-interest stakeholders | Fatigue and disengagement | Match frequency to stakeholder interest |
| Using technical jargon | Confusion and resistance | Use clear operational language |
| Sharing only problems | Negative perception | Share progress and quick wins |
| No feedback mechanism | Concerns remain unresolved | Encourage two-way communication |
| Inconsistent messaging | Confusion about priorities | Use centralized dashboards and updates |
