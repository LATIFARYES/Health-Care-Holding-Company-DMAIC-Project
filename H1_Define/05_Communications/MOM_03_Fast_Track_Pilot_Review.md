# Fast Track Pilot Scope

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Pilot Area | Emergency Department Fast Track Zone |
| Patient Type | Low-acuity / Minor cases |
| Pilot Duration | 2 Weeks |
| Pilot Shift | Evening Shift |
| Main Goal | Reduce congestion and improve patient flow |
| Expected Outcome | Faster treatment for minor cases and shorter waiting time for urgent patients |

---

# Current Operational Challenges Discussed

| Challenge | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| Minor cases occupying treatment beds | Delays urgent and critical cases |
| Peak-hour overcrowding | Increased Door-to-Doctor Time |
| Inconsistent triage routing | Workflow bottlenecks |
| Delayed admissions | Increased Length of Stay |
| Limited real-time queue visibility | Slow operational response |

---

# Proposed Fast Track Workflow

1. Triage identifies eligible low-acuity patients  
2. Patients routed directly to Fast Track area  
3. Dedicated physician and nurse assigned  
4. Rapid assessment and treatment process  
5. Discharge completed directly from Fast Track zone

# Risks & Concerns Raised

- Staffing shortages during evening shifts
- Potential confusion in patient routing during pilot startup
- Limited Fast Track treatment space
- Resistance to workflow adjustments
- IT dashboard integration delays

---

# Mitigation Actions

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|
| Staff resistance | Daily communication and shift huddles |
| Routing confusion | Clear signage and triage guidelines |
| Space limitations | Temporary dedicated Fast Track area |
| Dashboard delays | Manual backup tracking during pilot |
| Staffing pressure | Adjust staffing during peak hours |
