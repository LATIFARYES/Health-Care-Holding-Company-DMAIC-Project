What Is VOC? 🗣️

Voice of Customer (VOC) is the systematic process of:

Collecting patient and stakeholder feedback
Identifying recurring operational issues
Translating feedback into measurable CTQs
Prioritizing improvements based on patient impact and operational importance

Best Practices for VOC Collection ✅

Method 1: 

Patient Complaints Analysis
Review complaint records from the past 6–12 months
Categorize complaints:
Waiting time
Delays
Overcrowding
Communication issues
Use Pareto analysis to identify major complaint categories


Method 2: 

Direct Patient Interviews
Interview patients and family members
Use open-ended questions:
“What was the most frustrating part of your visit?”
Record exact patient statements without interpretation
Method 3: Patient Satisfaction Surveys
Keep surveys short and focused
Use rating scales (1–5)
Include at least one open-ended question


Method 4: 

Observation (Gemba Walk)
Observe patient flow directly inside the Emergency Department
Identify delays, bottlenecks, and congestion points
Document observations with notes and timing measurements


VOC Translation Rules 📌

Rule 1: Translate Needs, Not Solutions

❌ “Patients want more doctors” → solution

✅ “Patients need faster treatment” → customer need

Rule 2: Every CTQ Must Be Measurable

❌ “Improve waiting time”

✅ “Reduce Door-to-Doctor Time from 95 min to ≤45 min”

Rule 3: Validate CTQs with Patients

Do not rely only on internal assumptions.
Confirm whether CTQs accurately reflect patient expectations.


Rule 4: Prioritize Using Kano Model

| Category | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Must-Be | Basic expectation | Timely emergency treatment |
| Performance | Better performance increases satisfaction | Shorter waiting time |
| Delighter | Unexpected positive experience | Real-time waiting updates |


Common Mistakes ⚠️

| Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Collecting feedback only internally | Misses actual patient needs | Include real patients |
| Translating complaints into solutions | Weak root-cause analysis | Focus on needs first |
| CTQs without targets | Cannot measure success | Add measurable limits |
| Too many CTQs | Loss of focus | Prioritize top-impact CTQs |
| One-time VOC collection | Needs change over time | Review VOC continuously |
