**H1 — Define Phase**

Health Holding Company | Emergency Department DMAIC Project

Purpose ⤵️

The Define phase establishes the foundation of the Emergency Department improvement project. The objective is to clearly define the waiting-time problem, identify affected stakeholders, establish project boundaries, and define measurable success criteria before collecting data or implementing solutions.

**What Is the Define Phase? 📌**

The Define phase answers four key questions:

| Question ❓| Tool Used ⚒️| Output ⛳️|
|-----------|------------|---------|
| What is the problem? | Project Charter | Problem Statement, Goal Statement |
| What does the process look like? | SIPOC | High-level Emergency Department process map |
| What do patients need? | VOC → CTQ | Critical-to-Quality requirements |
| Who are the stakeholders? | Stakeholder Analysis | Engagement plan, communication plan |

**Key Findings (This Project) 🔍**

**Operational Inefficiency ‼️**

The Emergency Department is experiencing overcrowding and excessive waiting times. Current Door-to-Doctor Time averages 95 minutes against a target of ≤45 minutes. Delays are primarily caused by patient congestion, inefficient triage separation, and peak-hour staffing imbalance.

**Patient Flow Issues ⏳** 

Minor cases are consuming physician capacity intended for urgent and critical patients. The lack of an effective Fast Track pathway has created bottlenecks throughout the Emergency Department.

**Customer Impact 💡**

Patient satisfaction has decreased to 78% due to long waiting times, overcrowding, and delayed treatment.

**Staff Pressure 👩🏻‍⚕️👨🏻‍⚕️**

Medical staff are experiencing increased workload stress during peak hours due to inconsistent patient distribution and inefficient scheduling.


**Best Practices for the Define Phase ✅**

**1. Write a Strong Problem Statement**

A strong problem statement must clearly define ⤵️:

What is happening?
Where is it happening?
How large is the problem?
When does it occur?
What is NOT included?


**Good Example ✅**

“The Emergency Department has maintained an average Door-to-Doctor Time of 95 minutes since January 2026, exceeding the ≤45-minute target by more than 2×. This has contributed to overcrowding, increased patient dissatisfaction, and delays in critical patient care.”

**Bad Example ❌**

“The Emergency Department is slow because staffing is poor.”

**Key rule 🗃️:**
The Define phase describes the problem — not the cause and not the solution.


**2. Define SMART Goals 🎯**

| Element | Question | Example |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Specific | What will improve? | Reduce Emergency Department waiting time |
| Measurable | By how much? | From 95 min to ≤45 min |
| Achievable | Is it realistic? | Based on Fast Track benchmarks |
| Relevant | Does it impact operations? | Improves patient flow and satisfaction |
| Time-bound | By when? | Within 6 months |


**3. Keep the SIPOC at the Right Level 🗂️**

The SIPOC should:

Include only 5–7 major process steps
Focus on high-level Emergency Department flow
Show process boundaries clearly
Fit on one page


Example ED Process Steps
Patient Arrival
Registration
Triage
Medical Assessment
Treatment
Discharge / Admission


Common mistake:
Adding too much operational detail inside the SIPOC.


**4. Translate VOC Correctly 🧑🏻‍⚕️**

VOC → CTQ translates patient feedback into measurable requirements.

| Patient Feedback | Customer Need | CTQ |
|------------------|----------------|-----|
| “Waiting time is too long” | Faster treatment | Door-to-Doctor ≤45 min |
| “The ER is overcrowded” | Better patient flow | LOS ≤3 hours |
| “No clear queue visibility” | Transparency | Real-time waiting dashboard |
| “Critical cases are delayed” | Prioritization | Fast Track implementation |


**Rules for good CTQs:**

Must be measurable
Must have target values
Must reflect actual patient needs
Must be linked to KPIs


**5. Stakeholder Management 👥**
Stakeholders should be mapped based on Power and Interest.


| Quadrant | Power | Interest | Strategy |
|-----------|--------|----------|----------|
| Manage Closely | High | High | Frequent updates and involvement |
| Keep Satisfied | High | Low | Executive summaries |
| Keep Informed | Low | High | Share progress updates |
| Monitor | Low | Low | Minimal communication |

**Best practices 💡:**

Involve frontline staff early
Address resistance proactively
Maintain executive sponsorship
Document stakeholder concerns


**6. Common Mistakes to Avoid ⚠️


| Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Jumping directly to solutions | Weak root cause analysis | Focus on defining the problem first |
| Unclear problem statement | Cannot measure improvement | Use measurable KPIs |
| Ignoring patient feedback | Wrong priorities | Validate VOC data |
| Broad project scope | Delays and confusion | Use IS / IS NOT boundaries |
| Weak stakeholder engagement | Resistance to implementation | Use communication plans |
| No baseline metrics | Cannot prove improvement | Establish current-state data |


**Define Phase Checklist ✔️**

Before moving to the Measure phase:

Project Charter approved

Problem Statement quantified

SMART goals established

Scope boundaries defined

SIPOC completed

VOC collected from patients

CTQs identified and measurable

Stakeholders mapped

Communication plan established

Team roles assigned

Timeline approved
