**Define Stage**📍

This folder contains the complete "Define" phase artifacts for the Water Bottling Company process improvement project. The objective is to clearly frame the problem, establish measurable goals, understand the process boundaries, translate customer needs into CTQs, and identify all key stakeholders.

**Folder Contents** 🗂️

| Folder                     | Description                                              |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| 01_Project_Charter/       | Business case, SMART goals, team, and scope              |
| 02_Process_Mapping/       | SIPOC high-level process map                             |
| 03_Voice_Of_Customer/     | VOC translation and VOC-to-CTQ mapping                   |
| 04_Stakeholder_Analysis/  | Full stakeholder identification, mapping, engagement, and risk |


**Key Findings** ⛳️

**Operational Inefficiency**: The Emergency Department experiences prolonged door-to-doctor time averaging 95 minutes against a target of ≤45 minutes. Patient length of stay reaches 4.6 hours vs ≤3 hours, driven by delays in triage, registration, and bed availability.

**Quality of Care Issues**: Inconsistent triage prioritization and delays in clinical assessment increase the risk of complications, with high variability in treatment times and reduced adherence to clinical response standards.

**Patient Impact**: Approximately 8% of patients leave without being seen (target ≤2%), while patient satisfaction has dropped to 78% (target ≥90%), reflecting long wait times and poor communication.

**Internal Friction**: Medical and administrative staff face workload pressure and coordination challenges due to overcrowding, limited resources, and lack of real-time visibility on patient flow.
