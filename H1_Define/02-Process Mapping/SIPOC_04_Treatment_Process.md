
SIPOC 04 — Treatment Process

This SIPOC covers the treatment sub-process, where patients receive medical interventions, medications, monitoring, and stabilization. Delays in treatment execution, resource availability, and patient prioritization directly contribute to overcrowding and extended Length of Stay within the Emergency Department.


| Suppliers | Input | Process | Output | Customer |
|------------|--------|----------|---------|-----------|
| Emergency Physicians | Treatment orders, Diagnosis | 1. Treatment preparation | Ready treatment plan | Internal: Nursing Team |
| Nursing Team | Medications, Medical equipment | 2. Medication administration | Patient receiving treatment | Internal: Monitoring Area |
| Laboratory & Radiology | Diagnostic results | 3. Clinical intervention adjustment | Updated treatment actions | Internal: Emergency physician |
| Monitoring Systems | Vital signs, Patient condition | 4. Patient monitoring | Stabilized or escalated patient status | Internal: Admission / Discharge decision |
| Bed Management Team | Bed availability information | 5. Admission coordination | Assigned inpatient bed | Internal: Inpatient Departments |
| Emergency Department Staff | Completed treatment documentation | 6. Discharge preparation | Ready-for-discharge patient | External: Patients / Families |

Key Quality Concern ⚠️

Treatment delays caused by overcrowding, limited bed availability, delayed diagnostic results, and inefficient patient prioritization significantly increase Length of Stay and reduce Emergency Department capacity. Minor cases occupying treatment resources also reduce responsiveness to urgent and critical patients.
