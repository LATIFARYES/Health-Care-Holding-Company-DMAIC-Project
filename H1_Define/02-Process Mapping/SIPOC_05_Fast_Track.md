
SIPOC 05 — Fast Track

This SIPOC covers the Fast Track process designed for low-acuity and minor cases within the Emergency Department. The objective is to rapidly assess, treat, and discharge non-critical patients without consuming resources intended for urgent and critical cases.


| Suppliers | Input | Process | Output | Customer |
|------------|--------|----------|---------|-----------|
| Triage Team | Minor-case patient classification | 1. Fast Track patient identification | Eligible Fast Track patient | Internal: Fast Track Team |
| Registration System | Patient file, Queue number | 2. Fast Track registration confirmation | Verified Fast Track patient | Internal: Fast Track physician |
| Fast Track Physicians | Clinical guidelines, Patient symptoms | 3. Rapid medical assessment | Diagnosis and treatment plan | Internal: Nursing Team |
| Nursing Team | Medications, Treatment supplies | 4. Treatment and intervention | Treated patient | Internal: Discharge Process |
| QC / Documentation Team | Treatment documentation requirements | 5. Documentation and discharge preparation | Completed patient file | Internal: Discharge Team |
| Fast Track Staff | Discharge instructions, Follow-up plan | 6. Patient discharge | Discharged patient | External: Patients and Families |


Key Quality Concern ⚠️
Inefficient Fast Track routing, unclear triage criteria, and limited dedicated staffing reduce the effectiveness of the Fast Track pathway and increase congestion in the main Emergency Department treatment areas. Minor cases occupying physician capacity intended for urgent patients significantly contribute to prolonged Door-to-Doctor Time and overcrowding.
