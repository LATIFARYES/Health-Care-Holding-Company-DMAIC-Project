
SIPOC 03 — Medical Assessment

This SIPOC covers the medical assessment sub-process, where patients are evaluated by Emergency Department physicians to determine diagnosis, urgency, and treatment plans. Physician availability, assessment delays, and resource congestion are the primary operational risks at this stage.


| Suppliers | Input | Process | Output | Customer |
|------------|--------|----------|---------|-----------|
| Internal: Triage Team | Triage category, Patient file | 1. Patient assignment to physician | Assigned patient case | Internal: Emergency physician |
| Emergency Physicians | Clinical guidelines, Medical history | 2. Initial patient assessment | Preliminary diagnosis | Internal: Diagnostic services |
| Laboratory & Radiology | Lab requests, Imaging orders | 3. Diagnostic testing | Lab and imaging results | Internal: Emergency physician |
| Emergency Physicians | Diagnostic results, Patient symptoms | 4. Clinical decision-making | Treatment plan | Internal: Treatment Area |
| Nursing Team | Medication orders, Treatment instructions | 5. Treatment initiation | Patient receiving treatment | Internal: Monitoring / Discharge |
| Bed Management System | Bed availability, Admission criteria | 6. Admission or discharge decision | Patient disposition status | Internal: Admission / Discharge Team |


Key Quality Concern ⚠️

Delays in physician assessment, laboratory turnaround time, and bed availability significantly increase Door-to-Doctor Time and Length of Stay. Minor cases occupying physician capacity also contribute to congestion and delayed treatment for urgent and critical patients.
