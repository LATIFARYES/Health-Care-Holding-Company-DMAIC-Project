
SIPOC 02 — Triage Process

This SIPOC focuses on the triage sub-process, where patients are assessed and prioritized based on clinical urgency. Delays or inconsistencies at this stage directly affect Door-to-Doctor Time, patient flow efficiency, and the timely treatment of critical cases.

| Suppliers | Input | Process | Output | Customer |
|------------|--------|----------|---------|-----------|
| Patients | Symptoms, Medical complaints | 1. Patient arrival to triage | Waiting patient | Internal: Triage nurses |
| Registration Team | Registered patient file | 2. Patient verification | Verified patient information | Internal: Triage assessment |
| Nurses | Vital signs equipment, Triage protocol | 3. Vital signs assessment | Recorded patient vitals | Internal: Priority classification |
| Triage Nurses | Symptoms, Vital signs, Clinical guidelines | 4. Patient prioritization | Assigned triage level (1–5) | Internal: Emergency physicians |
| Queue Management System | Triage category data | 5. Queue assignment | Directed patient pathway | Internal: Fast Track / Treatment Area |
| Emergency Department Staff | Triage decision, Bed availability | 6. Patient transfer | Patient moved to treatment area | Internal: Medical Assessment |

Key Quality Concern ⚠️
Inconsistent triage classification, delays in vital-sign assessment, and overcrowding during peak hours significantly increase waiting times and delay treatment for urgent and critical patients. Minor cases occupying standard treatment pathways also reduce physician availability for high-priority cases.
