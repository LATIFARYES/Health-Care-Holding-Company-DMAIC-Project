
SIPOC 06 — Discharge, Admission & Patient Transfer

This SIPOC covers the final stage of the Emergency Department workflow — patient discharge, inpatient admission, or transfer to another care unit. Efficient coordination at this stage is critical to reducing overcrowding, improving bed availability, and maintaining patient flow throughout the Emergency Department.


| Suppliers | Input | Process | Output | Customer |
|------------|--------|----------|---------|-----------|
| Internal: Treatment Area | Completed treatment plan, Physician decision | 1. Discharge or admission decision | Final patient disposition | Internal: Bed Management / Discharge Team |
| Emergency Physicians | Admission orders, Discharge instructions | 2. Medical documentation completion | Completed medical records | Internal: Nursing Team |
| Nursing Team | Discharge education, Medication instructions | 3. Patient discharge preparation | Ready-for-discharge patient | External: Patients and Families |
| Bed Management Team | Bed availability information | 4. Inpatient bed assignment | Assigned hospital bed | Internal: Inpatient Departments |
| Transport Services | Patient transfer request | 5. Patient transfer coordination | Patient moved to inpatient unit | Internal: Hospital Units |
| Hospital Information System | Admission and discharge data | 6. System update and case closure | Updated patient status | External: Hospital Administration |


Key Quality Concern ⚠️

Delays in inpatient bed availability, incomplete discharge documentation, and slow transfer coordination significantly increase Emergency Department Length of Stay and contribute to overcrowding. Poor communication between departments also delays patient movement and reduces Emergency Department capacity during peak hours.
