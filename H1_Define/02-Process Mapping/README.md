02 — Process Mapping (SIPOC)

Health Holding Company | Define Phase

SIPOC is a high-level process map used to identify the Suppliers, Inputs, Process steps, Outputs, and Customers within the Emergency Department workflow. It defines the operational boundaries of the improvement project and helps visualize how patients move through the system.

**What Is SIPOC? 📑**


| Letter | Stands For | Question It Answers |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| S | Suppliers | Who provides inputs to the process? |
| I | Inputs | What resources or information enter the process? |
| P | Process | What are the major workflow steps? |
| O | Outputs | What does the process produce? |
| C | Customers | Who receives the outputs? |


**Why Use SIPOC in the Define Phase? 🎯**

Establishes Emergency Department process boundaries

Identifies internal and external stakeholders

Clarifies patient flow from arrival to discharge

Prevents scope creep during improvement activities

Helps visualize bottlenecks and delays


**SIPOC Summary — This Project 📊**

| Sub-Process | Key Input | Key Output | Primary Risk |
|--------------|------------|-------------|---------------|
| Registration | Patient information | Registered patient file | Delayed registration |
| Triage | Patient symptoms and vitals | Patient priority classification | Incorrect prioritization |
| Medical Assessment | Triage results | Diagnosis and treatment plan | Physician waiting delays |
| Treatment | Medical orders and resources | Stabilized patient | Treatment bottlenecks |
| Fast Track | Minor cases | Rapid patient discharge | Resource imbalance |
| Discharge / Admission | Completed treatment | Patient discharge or admission | Bed availability delays |


Note 📌

Triage and Medical Assessment are the highest-risk sub-processes because they contribute most significantly to the prolonged Door-to-Doctor Time and Emergency Department overcrowding.


Best Practices ✅

**Rule 1: Keep It High-Level**

Use only 5–7 major process steps

Focus on workflow summary, not detailed tasks

Each process step should use a verb + noun format

Example:
“Register Patient”
“Assess Patient”
“Provide Treatment”


**Rule 2: Build SIPOC in This Order**

Define the Process steps
Identify Outputs
Identify Customers
Define Inputs
Identify Suppliers


**Rule 3: Define Measurable Outputs**

Each output should include a measurable quality requirement.

Example:

“Patient assessed within ≤45 minutes”
“Triage completed within ≤5 minutes”

These outputs later become CTQs and KPIs.


**Rule 4: Include Internal Customers**

Each process stage becomes the customer of the previous stage.

**Example:**

Triage receives output from Registration
Physician Assessment receives output from Triage


**Common Mistakes ⚠️**

| Mistake | Why It Happens | How to Fix |
|----------|----------------|------------|
| Too many process steps | Excessive operational detail | Limit to 5–7 major steps |
| Unclear outputs | No measurable requirements | Add KPI-linked outputs |
| Missing internal customers | Focus only on patients | Map each handoff clearly |
| Confusing inputs and suppliers | Terminology confusion | Inputs = what enters, Suppliers = who provides |
| Using SIPOC as a flowchart | Misunderstanding the tool | Use separate detailed process maps |
