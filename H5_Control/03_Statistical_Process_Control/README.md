03 — Statistical Process Control (SPC)

Health Holding Company | Control Phase

Purpose: To monitor the stability and sustainability of Emergency Department operational improvements over time using Statistical Process Control (SPC) tools, distinguishing between normal operational variation and abnormal performance deterioration requiring intervention.

Control Charts Implemented 📊

SPC charts are used to continuously monitor the Emergency Department’s most critical operational KPIs.

1. X-Bar Control Chart (Average Performance)
Metric:

Door-to-Doctor Time

Data Source:

Electronic Health Record (EHR) timestamps and Queue Dashboard data

Purpose:

Monitor average physician waiting time and identify unusual increases in congestion.

Rule:

Any data point above the Upper Control Limit (UCL) triggers operational escalation and patient-flow review.

| SPC Chart | KPI Monitored | Frequency | Process Owner |
|------------|----------------|------------|----------------|
| X-Bar Chart | Door-to-Doctor Time | Hourly | ED Supervisor |

2. P-Chart (Proportion Nonconforming)
Metric:

LWBS (Left Without Being Seen) Rate

Data Source:

Patient tracking and discharge records

Purpose:

Monitor the proportion of patients leaving before physician assessment.

Rule:

Any increase above the established control limit triggers immediate congestion review and staffing escalation.

| SPC Chart | KPI Monitored | Frequency | Process Owner |
|------------|----------------|------------|----------------|
| P-Chart | LWBS Rate | Weekly | Quality Improvement Team |

3. Run Chart
Metric:

Triage Time

Data Source:

Triage workflow timestamps

Purpose:

Track triage process consistency and detect delays between shifts.

| SPC Chart | KPI Monitored | Frequency | Process Owner |
|------------|----------------|------------|----------------|
| Run Chart | Triage Time | Shift-based | Nursing Supervisor |

4. Trend Chart
Metric:

Length of Stay (LOS)

Data Source:

Patient admission and discharge records

Purpose:

Monitor long-term Emergency Department congestion trends and admission delays.

| SPC Chart | KPI Monitored | Frequency | Process Owner |
|------------|----------------|------------|----------------|
| Trend Chart | Length of Stay (LOS) | Daily | ED Leadership |

SPC Escalation Rules 🚨

| SPC Signal | Required Action |
|-------------|----------------|
| Point above UCL | Immediate operational escalation |
| 7 consecutive upward points | Workflow review required |
| Sudden spike in variation | Root Cause Analysis (RCA) initiated |
| Persistent LOS increase | Bed escalation coordination |
| LWBS trend increase | Activate congestion response plan |

Operational Monitoring Dashboard 🏥

The SPC system is integrated into the Emergency Department operational dashboard to provide:

Real-time KPI visibility
Early warning signals
Congestion trend monitoring
Shift-to-shift performance comparison
Escalation alerts


Key Benefits of SPC ✅

Detect operational deterioration early
Prevent return to old workflows
Sustain Improve phase gains
Support data-driven operational decisions
Improve consistency between shifts
