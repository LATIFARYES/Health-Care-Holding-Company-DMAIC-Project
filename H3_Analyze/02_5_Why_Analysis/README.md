02 — 5-Why Analysis
Health Holding Company | Analyze Phase

The 5-Why technique is used to identify the true root causes behind Emergency Department delays and overcrowding by repeatedly asking “Why?” until the controllable root cause is identified.

5-Why Results — This Project 🚨

| لماذا؟ | السؤال | التوجيه التحليلي | الإجابة |
|---------|----------|------------------|----------|
| لماذا 1 | لماذا يتجاوز وقت الوصول للطبيب 45 دقيقة؟ | ابدأ بالسبب الأكثر وضوحاً من البيانات | لأن 75% من المرضى ينتظرون أكثر من 45 دقيقة للدخول على الدكتور |
| لماذا 2 | لماذا يحدث هذا السبب؟ | هل يرتبط بالوردية؟ بمستوى الخطورة؟ بعدد الكوادر؟ | يلاحظ زيادة متوسط فترة الانتظار حسب مستوى الخطورة |
| لماذا 3 | لماذا يحدث السبب الثاني؟ | تعمق أكثر في البيانات | بسبب عدم وجود آلية واضحة للفرز |
| لماذا 4 | لماذا يحدث السبب الثالث؟ | هل يرتبط بالطاقة الاستيعابية أم بالإجراءات؟ | يرتبط بالإجراءات |
| لماذا 5 | ما السبب الجذري النهائي؟ | يجب أن يكون قابلاً للتحكم ومدعوماً بالبيانات | غياب مسار واضح لتحديد البروتوكول |


Chain 1: Door-to-Doctor Time Exceeds 45 Minutes

| Why # | Question | Answer |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Why 1 | Why does Door-to-Doctor Time exceed 45 minutes? | 75% of patients wait more than 45 minutes before physician assessment |
| Why 2 | Why are patients waiting this long? | Waiting times increase significantly during evening shifts and vary by triage level |
| Why 3 | Why do evening shifts have longer waiting times? | 60% of evening-shift patients require laboratory investigations, and no clear patient-routing process exists |
| Why 4 | Why is there no clear patient-routing process? | Minor and critical cases follow the same operational workflow |
| Why 5 | Why are all patients following the same workflow? | Lack of Fast Track pathway and insufficient staffing during evening peak hours |
| Root Cause |  | No Fast Track system and insufficient peak-hour staffing coverage |


Lean Connection: 5-Why + Fast Track 🏥

The 5-Why analysis confirms that overcrowding and delays are strongly linked to workflow design and staffing imbalance.

| Improvement Area | Action | Expected Impact |
|------------------|--------|-----------------|
| Fast Track Pathway | Separate minor cases from urgent cases | Reduce congestion and waiting time |
| Smart Staffing | Increase staffing during evening peak hours | Faster patient assessment |
| Standardized Triage | Clear routing protocol by acuity level | Reduce workflow variation |
| Real-Time Queue Monitoring | Live waiting-time visibility | Faster operational response |


Best Practices ✅
Practice 1: Use Data at Every Step

Each answer must be supported by operational data, observations, or KPI analysis.

Example:

Evening shifts show highest waiting times
60% of evening patients require lab investigations
Practice 2: Stop at the Actionable Root Cause

The goal is not exactly five questions.

Stop when:

The cause is controllable
The organization can act on it
Practice 3: Separate Symptoms from Causes

❌ “Waiting times are high” → symptom

✅ “No Fast Track process exists” → root cause

Practice 4: Involve Frontline Teams

The most accurate answers usually come from:

Triage nurses
Emergency physicians
Charge nurses
Bed management staff

Common Mistakes ⚠️

| Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Using opinions instead of data | Incorrect root causes | Validate using KPI analysis |
| Blaming people instead of process | Resistance to improvement | Focus on workflow and systems |
| Stopping too early | Surface-level causes only | Continue until actionable cause |
| Mixing solutions with causes | Weak analysis | Separate analysis from improvement |
| Conducting analysis without frontline staff | Missing operational insight | Include operational teams |
