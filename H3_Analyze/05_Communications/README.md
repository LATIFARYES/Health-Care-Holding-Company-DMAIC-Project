Root Cause Confirmation Update

Dear Hospital Management,

We have successfully completed the Analyze phase of the Emergency Department DMAIC Improvement Project.

Using operational data analysis, hypothesis testing, Fishbone analysis, and 5-Why sessions, the project team has confirmed several key root causes contributing to Emergency Department overcrowding and delays.

Confirmed root causes include:

1. Lack of Fast Track process for low-acuity patients
2. Staffing shortages during evening peak hours
3. Delays in inpatient admissions and bed allocation
4. Limited real-time visibility of patient queues and waiting times
5. Workflow variation between shifts and triage processes

Key findings also confirmed that:

* Evening shifts experience significantly higher waiting times
* Minor cases contribute heavily to congestion
* Admission delays directly increase Length of Stay (LOS)

The project team will now transition into the Improve phase to design and pilot targeted operational solutions.

A detailed Analyze Phase report and supporting KPI analysis will be shared separately for review.

Thank you for your continued support and engagement throughout this initiative.

Best regards,
