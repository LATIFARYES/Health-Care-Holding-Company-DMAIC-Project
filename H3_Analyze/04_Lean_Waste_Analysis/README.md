04 — Lean Waste Analysis (DOWNTIME)
Health Holding Company | Analyze Phase

Lean Waste Analysis identifies non-value-adding activities that contribute to Emergency Department overcrowding, long waiting times, delays in patient flow, and operational inefficiency.

This analysis connects Lean waste directly to the confirmed root causes identified during Fishbone, 5-Why, and Hypothesis Testing.

The 8 Wastes (DOWNTIME) — Detailed Results 🏥
T — Transport

| Observation | Current State | Impact |
|--------------|---------------|--------|
| Patients moved between multiple waiting areas | Repeated patient transfers | Increased waiting and congestion |
| Nurses walk between distant stations repeatedly | Long movement distance | Delays in patient response |
| Laboratory requests require physical coordination | Additional transport delays | Increased LOS |

Lean Solution: Redesign patient-flow layout and centralize key services.

I — Inventory

| Observation | Current State | Impact |
|--------------|---------------|--------|
| Large queue of low-acuity patients | High waiting-room backlog | Congestion during peak hours |
| Admission holds occupy treatment beds | Bed shortages | Delayed treatment capacity |
| Waiting patients accumulate during evening shifts | Overloaded system | Increased Door-to-Doctor Time |

Lean Solution: Fast Track pathway + improved admission coordination.

M — Motion

| Observation | Current State | Impact |
|--------------|---------------|--------|
| Staff walk long distances between stations | Repeated movement | Lost operational time |
| Physicians move between overcrowded zones | Workflow disruption | Delayed assessments |
| Nurses search for available beds manually | Process delays | Increased patient waiting |
Lean Solution: 5S implementation and optimized department layout.


W — Waiting


| Observation | Current State | Impact |
|--------------|---------------|--------|
| Patients wait for physician assessment | Average 95 min | Delayed care |
| Patients wait for inpatient beds | Admission bottleneck | Increased LOS |
| Delays in laboratory and imaging turnaround | Treatment delays | Congestion escalation |
| Staff wait for room availability | Idle operational time | Reduced throughput |


Total Waiting Waste: Major contributor to Emergency Department overcrowding.

Lean Solution: Fast Track + smart staffing + real-time queue visibility.

O — Overproduction

| Observation | Current State | Impact |
|--------------|---------------|--------|
| Duplicate documentation activities | Unnecessary workload | Reduced staff efficiency |
| Repeated triage reassessments | Process duplication | Increased delays |

Lean Solution: Standardized workflow and simplified documentation process.

O — Over-Processing

| Observation | Current State | Impact |
|--------------|---------------|--------|
| Duplicate patient registration steps | Extra processing time | Slower patient flow |
| Multiple approval steps for admissions | Administrative delay | Extended LOS |
| Manual reporting across systems | Repeated data entry | Operational waste |

Lean Solution: Workflow simplification and system integration.

D — Defects

| Defect Type | Impact |
|--------------|--------|
| Incorrect triage prioritization | Delayed critical cases |
| Delayed physician assignment | Long Door-to-Doctor Time |
| Incomplete patient routing | Workflow bottlenecks |
| Queue visibility gaps | Slow operational response |

Lean Solution: Standardized triage and dashboard monitoring.

S — Skills (Underutilized Talent)

| Observation | Impact |
|--------------|--------|
| Frontline staff not involved in workflow redesign | Resistance to change |
| Limited operational feedback from nurses | Missed improvement opportunities |
| No structured continuous-improvement culture | Slow operational learning |

Lean Solution: Kaizen workshops and frontline involvement.
