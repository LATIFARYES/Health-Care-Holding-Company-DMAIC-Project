01 — Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa)
Health Holding Company | Analyze Phase

The Fishbone Diagram is a structured brainstorming tool used to identify all possible causes contributing to Emergency Department overcrowding, delays, and long patient waiting times.

The Fishbone generates hypotheses — not conclusions.
All suspected causes must later be validated using operational data and statistical analysis.

The 6M Framework 🐟

| Category | Covers | Emergency Department Example |
|-----------|--------|------------------------------|
| Man | People, staffing, training, behavior | Staffing shortages, triage experience variation |
| Machine | Systems, equipment, technology | Slow system response, limited dashboard visibility |
| Method | Processes, workflows, procedures | No Fast Track workflow, inconsistent triage process |
| Material | Supplies, rooms, resources | Limited treatment rooms and bed availability |
| Measurement | KPI tracking, monitoring accuracy | No real-time queue monitoring |
| Mother Nature | Environment, external conditions | Peak-hour overcrowding, seasonal patient surges |


Fishbone Results — Emergency Department Delays 🚨
Man (People)
Staffing shortages during peak hours
Variation in triage decisions between nurses
Limited communication during shift handovers
Physicians overloaded by low-acuity cases
Inconsistent workflow practices between shifts


Machine (Systems & Technology)
No real-time patient queue dashboard
Delays in electronic documentation systems
Limited visibility of bed availability
Slow reporting and operational tracking systems
No automated alert system for overcrowding


Method (Processes)
No standardized Fast Track process
Minor cases follow the same pathway as critical patients
Delays in inpatient admission workflow
Inconsistent triage prioritization process
Lack of standardized escalation process during congestion



Material (Resources)
Limited treatment rooms during peak periods
Bed shortages in inpatient departments
Insufficient staffing during evening shifts
Limited Fast Track capacity and resources



Measurement
No live monitoring of waiting times
Delayed KPI reporting
Inconsistent data collection between shifts
No real-time visibility of queue status


Mother Nature (Environment)
Evening and weekend patient surges
Seasonal increases in patient arrivals
Overcrowding during public holidays
Physical Emergency Department layout limitations
